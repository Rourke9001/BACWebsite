Questions to Ask Your Air Freight Provider | BAC

2 April 2026

Tags: Questions to Ask Your Air Freight Provider Before Shipping

Air freight is an essential service for businesses needing fast and reliable transportation of goods across the globe. However, choosing the right air freight provider can be a daunting task, given the complexities of international shipping and logistics. To ensure your goods are handled with care and arrive on time, it’s crucial to ask the right questions before committing to a provider. 

 

Here are the key questions to guide your decision-making process, when appointing an Air Freight provider:

 

1. What Shipping Options Do You Offer?

Understanding the range of services provided is critical. Ask about their options for:

  • Express vs. Standard Shipping: Determine whether they offer varying speeds of delivery based on urgency.

  • Door-to-Door vs. Airport-to-Airport Services: Clarify the level of convenience they provide.

  • Handling of Special Cargo: Check if they accommodate fragile, oversized, hazardous, or temperature-controlled goods.

This will help you assess if their offerings align with your specific shipping requirements.

 

2. What Are Your Pricing Structures and Hidden Costs?

Cost transparency is vital to avoid unpleasant surprises. Ask about:

  • Base rates and additional fees (e.g., fuel surcharges, customs fees, or security charges).

  • Volume discounts or flexible pricing for regular shipments.

  • Cancellation and rebooking policies.

A clear understanding of costs will help you manage your budget effectively.

 

3. How Do You Ensure the Security of Shipments?

Air freight often involves valuable goods, making security a top concern. Enquire about:

  • Screening processes for shipments.

  • Measures to prevent theft or damage.

  • Use of tracking systems and real-time updates.

A provider that prioritises security and transparency ensures peace of mind.

 

4. What Are Your Transit Times and Delivery Guarantees?

Time-sensitive shipments demand reliable schedules. Ask:

  • Expected transit times for various destinations.

  • Procedures in place for delays or missed deadlines.

  • Guarantee policies in case of late deliveries.

This will help you plan your logistics and meet customer expectations efficiently.

 

5. What Documentation Support Do You Provide?

International shipping involves extensive paperwork. Confirm whether the provider assists with:

  • Export declarations and airway bills.

  • Customs clearance and compliance documentation.

  • Certificates for special goods (e.g., hazardous materials or perishables).

Providers that simplify documentation processes save you time and effort.

 

6. Do You Have Experience in My Industry?

Certain industries, such as pharmaceuticals, electronics, or perishable goods, require specialised handling. Ask:

  • Whether they have experience with your type of cargo.

  • If they can provide references from similar clients.

  • How they manage industry-specific challenges.

Experienced providers are better equipped to meet unique requirements.

 

7. What Is Your Track Record for Reliability?

A reliable provider is key to avoiding disruptions. Request details on:

  • On-time delivery rates.

  • Customer satisfaction metrics.

  • Handling of disputes and claims.

This information will help you gauge their performance and trustworthiness.

 

8. What Insurance Coverage Do You Offer?

Shipping goods by air involves risks, making insurance non-negotiable. Clarify:

  • The extent of their coverage.

  • Whether they provide all-risk insurance or limited liability.

  • Procedures for filing claims in case of damage or loss.

Adequate insurance ensures you’re protected against potential financial losses.

 

9. How Do You Handle Customs and Regulatory Compliance?

Non-compliance can result in fines, delays, or confiscation of goods. Confirm:

  • Their knowledge of international trade regulations.

  • Processes for customs clearance.

  • Support in handling tariffs, duties, and taxes.

A provider well-versed in compliance minimises risks and ensures smoother shipping.

 

10. What Technologies Do You Use to Improve Service?

Modern logistics relies heavily on technology for efficiency. Enquire about:

  • Real-time shipment tracking.

  • Automated notifications and updates.

  • Use of AI or predictive analytics to prevent delays.

Providers leveraging advanced technology can offer superior service and reliability.

 

11. What Happens If Something Goes Wrong?

Delays, damage, or loss can occur even with the best planning. Ask:

  • Their protocols for addressing disruptions.

  • Customer support availability and responsiveness.

  • Compensation policies for missed deadlines or damaged goods.

A responsive provider with a clear plan for contingencies ensures better outcomes.

 

12. How Do You Communicate During the Shipping Process?

Effective communication is critical for successful shipping. Ask:

  • How often they provide updates.

  • Preferred communication channels (e.g., email, phone, or online portals).

  • Dedicated account managers or support teams.

Clear communication ensures you’re always informed about your shipment’s status.

 

Conclusion on Questions to ask when appointing an Air Freight provider

 

Selecting the right air freight provider requires careful consideration and thorough questioning. By addressing these key areas, you can ensure that your chosen partner aligns with your business needs, guarantees reliability, and offers value for your investment.
