Making the Most of Your Air Freight Budget | BAC

2 April 2026

Tags: Balancing Speed and Cost: Making the Most of Your Air Freight Budget

In the fast-paced world of global trade, air freight stands out as a preferred shipping method for its speed and reliability. However, it often comes at a higher cost compared to other freight options. For businesses looking to optimise their supply chain, striking a balance between speed and cost is essential. This article explores how to maximise your air freight budget effectively, with insights tailored to BAC Logistics’ expertise in air freight services.

 

Maximising your Air Freight Budget

Understanding Air Freight Costs and Their Key Components

Air freight costs are influenced by several factors that businesses must understand to manage their budgets effectively. These include:

  • Weight-Based Pricing: Air freight charges are calculated based on either the actual weight or volumetric weight of the shipment, whichever is higher.
  • Fuel Surcharges: Airlines impose additional charges to offset fluctuating fuel prices.
  • Customs and Security Fees: Import and export fees, including documentation, contribute significantly to the overall cost.
  • Handling Charges: Costs associated with the loading, unloading, and storage of cargo.

By understanding these components, businesses can forecast expenses more accurately and identify potential areas for cost optimisation.

 

Factors Influencing Air Freight Speed

When time is of the essence, understanding the variables that impact air freight speed is critical:

  • Shipment Size and Weight: Smaller shipments often enjoy faster handling, while larger consignments may require special arrangements.
  • Customs Clearance: Delays in documentation or compliance can significantly slow down transit times.
  • Route and Carrier Selection: Direct flights and reputable carriers generally ensure quicker delivery times.

By aligning shipment requirements with carrier capabilities, businesses can reduce transit delays and achieve timely delivery.

 

Balancing Speed and Cost: Finding the Right Trade-Off

Determining when to prioritise speed over cost, or vice versa, depends on the nature of the goods being shipped and business needs:

  • Urgent Deliveries: Perishable goods, medical supplies, or critical components may justify higher costs for expedited delivery.
  • Non-Urgent Shipments: For less time-sensitive goods, opting for cost-effective solutions such as deferred air freight can save money.

BAC Logistics helps businesses make informed decisions by offering tailored solutions that meet specific requirements without compromising quality.

Tips for Reducing Air Freight Costs Without Compromising Speed

Businesses can implement several strategies to make air freight more cost-efficient while maintaining timely deliveries:

  1. Consolidate Shipments: Grouping smaller shipments into one larger consignment can lower per-unit costs.
  2. Plan Around Peak Periods: Avoid shipping during high-demand times, such as holidays, to reduce costs.
  3. Ensure Accurate Documentation: Minimise delays and associated penalties by preparing complete and compliant paperwork.
