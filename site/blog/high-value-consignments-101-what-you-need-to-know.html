High-Value Consignments 101 | BAC Logistics

2 April 2026

Tags: High-Value Consignments 101: What You Need to Know

Introduction

When transporting high-value consignments, businesses face unique challenges that require careful planning, security, and compliance with international regulations. Whether it's luxury goods, electronics, medical equipment, or sensitive data storage devices, ensuring these shipments reach their destination safely and on time is a top priority.

As a global logistics provider based in South Africa, BAC Logistics specializes in the secure and efficient transportation of high-value consignments. This guide explores everything you need to know about shipping high-value items, the risks involved, and how to mitigate them effectively.

 

Understanding High-Value Consignments

 

High-value consignments refer to shipments with significant financial worth, sensitive intellectual property, or items that are highly desirable to thieves. These can include:

  • Precious metals, gemstones, and jewellery

  • High-end electronics and technology

  • Medical and pharmaceutical products

  • Luxury fashion and designer goods

  • Confidential documents and sensitive data storage devices

  • Industrial machinery and specialized equipment

Due to their high worth, these consignments require additional security measures, careful handling, and compliance with strict customs regulations.

 

Key Risks in High-Value Shipping

1. Theft and Pilferage

High-value items are a prime target for theft. From opportunistic pilfering at warehouses to organized crime at transport hubs, inadequate security can lead to significant losses.

2. Damage in Transit

Fragile or expensive equipment can suffer damage if not properly packed, handled, or transported under optimal conditions.

3. Delays and Customs Complications

Incorrect paperwork, regulatory non-compliance, or customs clearance issues can lead to costly delays, especially for international shipments.

4. Counterfeit or Fraudulent Activities

Luxury and high-tech products are often subject to counterfeiting and fraud, making secure handling and tracking essential.

5. Cybersecurity Threats (for Data Storage Devices and Intellectual Property)

In the digital age, sensitive data and intellectual property stored in physical drives or equipment can be compromised during transit.

Best Practices for Securing High-Value Consignments

1. Choose a Trusted Logistics Provider

Working with a reliable logistics partner like BAC Logistics ensures that your shipment is handled by professionals with experience in secure transportation.

2. Invest in Secure Packaging

High-value goods require durable, tamper-proof packaging with advanced security features such as:

  • Shock-resistant materials for fragile items

  • GPS-enabled containers for tracking

  • Tamper-evident seals to detect unauthorized access

3. Use Real-Time Tracking and Monitoring

Advanced GPS and RFID tracking technology allows for real-time monitoring of consignments. This provides:

  • Immediate updates on shipment location

  • Alerts for any route deviations or security breaches

  • Enhanced visibility for clients

4. Obtain Comprehensive Cargo Insurance

Cargo insurance mitigates financial losses in case of theft, damage, or unforeseen circumstances. Ensure your insurance policy covers:

  • Full value of the goods

  • Various transport modes (air, sea, and road)

  • High-risk zones and international shipping

5. Ensure Compliance with Regulations

For international shipments, regulatory compliance is critical. This includes:

  • Proper classification and documentation

  • Adhering to customs requirements and trade restrictions

  • Following international security standards (e.g., TAPA – Transported Asset Protection Association guidelines)

6. Implement Multi-Layered Security Measures

For added security, use:

  • Secure warehouses with 24/7 surveillance

  • Escort services for high-risk shipments

  • Special handling teams trained for valuable goods

7. Plan for Emergency Contingencies

Despite best efforts, unforeseen circumstances can arise. A well-structured contingency plan should include:

  • Alternate transport routes

  • Emergency response teams

  • Communication protocols for disruptions
