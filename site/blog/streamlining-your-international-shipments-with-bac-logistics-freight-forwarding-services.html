What is Freight Forwarding?

Freight forwarding is the coordination and management of the movement of goods from one destination to another via various transportation methods - air, sea, or road. A freight forwarder acts as the intermediary between shippers and carriers, ensuring that cargo gets from point A to point B as smoothly and cost-effectively as possible.

The key roles of a freight forwarder include:

  • Negotiating transport rates

  • Booking cargo space

  • Handling documentation and customs

  • Managing storage and warehousing

  • Coordinating local delivery and last-mile logistics
