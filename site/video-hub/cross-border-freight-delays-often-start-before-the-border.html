Cross-Border Freight Delays Often Start Before the Border

2 July 2026

Tags: Cross-Border Transport Cross-Border Freight, Road Freight, SADC Logistics, Southern African Logistics,

Cross-border transport in Southern Africa is not only about arranging a truck. For businesses moving goods across borders, the success of the movement depends on what is prepared before the cargo leaves.

In this video, BAC Logistics explains why cross-border freight often comes under pressure before the truck reaches the border. Unclear cargo details, incomplete documents, customs uncertainty, poor timing, supplier delays and communication gaps can all affect delivery timelines once the cargo is already in motion.

The video highlights what businesses should confirm before dispatch, including accurate cargo information, documentation readiness, customs requirements, route planning, border timing, supplier readiness and receiving arrangements.

At BAC Logistics, we support local and cross-border road freight across South Africa and key Southern African trade corridors. Our approach focuses on planning the full movement, not only supplying a vehicle, so that cargo, documents, customs requirements, route coordination and delivery expectations are aligned before dispatch.

Planning cargo movement across Southern Africa? Speak to BAC Logistics about local and cross-border transport built around route planning, customs readiness, documentation alignment and accountable delivery.
